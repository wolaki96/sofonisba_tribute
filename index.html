Sofonisba Anguissola

1532-1625

About Sofonisba

  • Sofonisba Anguissola was born in Cremona in 1532
  • She was the oldest of seven children and 6 girls
  • Anguissola was born into a noble background
  • Her father went against the mold of Italian training for women at the time, and educated all of his daughters
  • When Sofonisba was 14, her father sent her to the Lombard School to learn how to paint
  • When she was 22, she was introduced to Michelangelo in Rome and was guided by his advice
  • She was not allowed to study anatomy due to being a woman, so she practiced doing many self-portraits and portraits of family members
  • In 1559 she became the lady in waiting and art teacher to Queen Elisabeth of Valois in Spain
  • Anguissola did not marry until she was in her 40s due to the urging of the King of Spain
  • Her paintings are still highly regarded and can be seen all over Europe and the United States
